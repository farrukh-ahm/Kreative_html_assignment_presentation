Gandalf

Gandalf the Grey, later known as Gandalf the White, is a wise and powerful wizard in J.R.R. Tolkien's "The Lord of the Rings." Serving as a guide and mentor to the protagonists, Gandalf plays a crucial role in the quest to defeat the Dark Lord Sauron. Possessing immense magical abilities and deep knowledge of Middle-earth's history, Gandalf provides invaluable counsel and support to the Fellowship of the Ring. His unwavering commitment to the forces of good, coupled with his compassion and bravery, makes him a revered figure and a beacon of hope in the struggle against darkness.
